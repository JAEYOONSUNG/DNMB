# Type I HsdS recognition prediction validation

This validation records the frozen Type I-S benchmarks, the successive spacer
ablations, and the final DNMB production rule. It is intended to prevent the
self-hit and duplicate-row leakage that affected the earlier DefenseViz
prototype and to keep structural-model gains attributable to the spacer branch.

## Reference and cohort

- Official REBASE file: `Type_I_S_subunit_Gold_Standards_Protein.txt`
- Download URL: `https://rebase.neb.com/rebase/Type_I_S_subunit_Gold_Standards_Protein.txt`
- File SHA-256: `f33b4ce929e751bfd16ac3659531a38e0434ad7f307b7edba1740f8cecc2b96e`
- Downloaded: 2026-07-15
- Raw records: 1,016
- Canonical full-length, simple bipartite, exact-protein-deduplicated records
  produced by parser `type1s-v1.2.0`: 689
- Production search database: `separate-trd-v1.2.0`
- Production prediction rules: `type1s-predict-v1.4.0`
- Spacer model: `scaffold-ensemble-tael-v1.1.0`

Motifs were parsed by selecting the longest central `N` run of length 3-15.
This preserves `N` symbols that belong to a half-site. Protein sequences were
cleaned before length calculation and search. Identical protein sequences were
collapsed before splitting.

## Frozen split and leakage controls

- Split seed: `20260715`
- Development split: 346 proteins
- Untouched test split: 343 proteins
- A query's full reference record was removed from its TRD1, TRD2,
  cross-position, whole-protein, and scaffold searches.
- A cross-position hit with the same full-reference ID was also removed.
- Search: DIAMOND sensitive mode; identity >=25%, alignment >=35 aa,
  query/subject coverage >=30%, E-value <=1e-3.
- Accuracy in the TSV is conditional precision among emitted predictions;
  coverage is always reported separately.

## Reproducibility audit

`reproduce_type1s_benchmark.R` is the executable provenance record for the
final exact-LOO and TRD90 results. It requires the official Gold file and
DIAMOND; it does not download a substitute or use synthetic sequences. The
script verifies the frozen source SHA-256 and production parser/model versions,
parses all 689 references, builds fresh TRD1, TRD2, whole-HsdS, and scaffold
DIAMOND databases, and performs all-versus-all searches. Exact-LOO removes the
query reference from every search. TRD90 first reconstructs the same- and
cross-position TRD graph, verifies 550 components (498 singletons; maximum 30),
and removes the complete query component before selecting the per-database top
30. The final predictions are made by the production DNMB selector, spacer
ensemble, TAEL-ruler, and confidence functions rather than by copied benchmark
logic.

Run it from the DNMB checkout, for example:

```bash
Rscript inst/validation/reproduce_type1s_benchmark.R \
  --gold ~/.dnmb-cache/db_modules/rebasefinder/cache/Type_I_S_subunit_Gold_Standards_Protein.txt \
  --output-dir /tmp/dnmb-type1s-audit --cpu 8
```

The audit fails on a source/version/graph mismatch and compares regenerated
exact-LOO, TRD90, deterministic development/test, high-confidence, and four
TAEL-case counts with the committed TSVs. It also checks the arithmetic of all
aggregate validation tables. `type1s_benchmark_predictions.tsv` retains the
1,378 per-query decisions used by the aggregate audit, while
`type1s_benchmark_provenance.tsv` records the machine-readable protocol and
source identity.

Before any database build or search, the audit also requires the installed
DIAMOND version string to match `benchmark_diamond_version` exactly. The two
legacy high-confidence evaluable denominators are read as required positive
integers from `legacy_v1_high_validation_evaluable_n` and
`legacy_v1_high_test_evaluable_n`; they are not embedded as script constants.

The physically separated databases recovered one extra weak, incorrect R0285
TRD1 call after the first common half-site cohort had been frozen. The audit
retains both its operational call and the documented frozen suppression, so
the 675/663/650 call denominators are explicit rather than reconstructed from
the aggregate values. The earlier `type1s_gold_results.tsv` v1 checkpoint has
no retained per-query search output and is therefore arithmetic-audited, not
claimed to be regenerated by the v1.4 production engine. Its high-only row
exactly implies 345 development and 342 test evaluable records; that legacy
denominator is recorded separately from the final 346/343 split.

This exact-sequence leave-one-protein-out benchmark measures practical
reference-homology performance. It is not a claim of de novo family-holdout
generalization, so it is paired with the stricter TRD-cluster holdout reported
below.

## Compared methods

The design follows experimentally established Type I HsdS biology: each of the
two tandem TRDs specifies one half of the bipartite recognition sequence, and
N/C cross-position homologs bind complementary orientations. See the
[Type I review](https://academic.oup.com/nar/article/42/1/20/2435301) and the
[StySKI cross-position experiment](https://academic.oup.com/nar/article/25/9/1694/2901964).
REBASE itself documents Gold-Standard-based annotation propagation and
similarity matching, but does not disclose a TRD1/TRD2 database algorithm in
its [2023 database paper](https://academic.oup.com/nar/article/51/D1/D629/6786197).

The baseline reproduces the old proportional windows (TRD1 10-40%, TRD2
58-92%) and spacer formula `round(4.2 + 0.025 * linker_length)`.

Type I-S v1 uses validation-selected specificity-core windows (TRD1 11-30%,
TRD2 66-82%), position-aware and reverse-complement-aware cross-TRD search,
and a three-tier spacer rule: close agreeing TRDs first, a close whole-HsdS
nearest neighbor second, and otherwise a reciprocal-coverage-weighted vote.
Version 1.4 retains those two close-homolog gates and replaces only the remote
fallback with a 70:30 ensemble of the five closest whole-HsdS homologs and 20
closest conserved-scaffold homologs. Version 1.4 then applies a narrow Type-IC
TAEL-repeat molecular-ruler correction only to canonical two-repeat/N6 versus
three-repeat/N7 reference pairs that pass the gates specified below.

The production database layout is physically separated into TRD1, TRD2,
whole-HsdS, and spacer-scaffold FASTA/search databases. Both TRD query windows
are searched against both position databases, so an N-terminal query can still
reuse a homologous C-terminal TRD (and conversely) after IUPAC
reverse-complement orientation. The spacer scaffold concatenates residues
outside the two specificity cores: 1-11%, 30-66%, and 82-100% after the same
rounded boundaries. It therefore represents conserved N-terminal, central
coiled-coil, and distal regions rather than a predicted three-dimensional
distance. The fixed windows are validation-selected coordinates, not claimed
to be experimentally curated domain boundaries.

REBASE documents Gold-Standard-based propagation and similarity matches for
putative Type I systems, but it does not publish whether its internal Best
Evidence Algorithm uses separate TRD databases. This implementation is
therefore a biologically motivated, independently validated design—not a claim
to reproduce undisclosed REBASE internals.

## Separate-database integrity

Database version `separate-trd-v1.2.0` contains 689 labeled records per
position after exact full-protein deduplication:

- TRD1: 657 unique protein windows; zero half-site conflicts; one spacer-label
  conflict group.
- TRD2: 655 unique protein windows; zero half-site conflicts; one spacer-label
  conflict group.

The metadata records the complete spacer set for every identical protein
window. A conflicting spacer set is never used for TRD-source consensus; it
falls back to the nearest whole-HsdS Gold protein. With the final close-homolog
gate, this guard blocked three exact-LOO TRD-consensus selections.
Separate per-position top-30 searches recovered cross-position candidates that
a single combined top-30 search had truncated in 48/1,378 exact-LOO query TRDs
and 24/1,378 TRD90 query TRDs. The final one-neighbor selections were otherwise
unchanged by physical separation alone.

The v1.2 database adds 689 spacer-scaffold records. Its metadata stores the
three source-coordinate segments, scaffold and central lengths,
helix-friendly `(A,E,K,L,Q,R)` and helix-breaker `(P,G)` fractions, longest
exact tandem four-residue repeat, TAEL-like repeat count, recognition motif,
and model version. These diagnostics make the structural proxy auditable; the
production classifier itself uses scaffold sequence homology rather than a
hard-coded helix-length formula.

## Conserved-scaffold and TAEL molecular-ruler spacer model

Structural work on HsdS shows that conserved antiparallel alpha helices form a
molecular ruler between the two TRDs. The classic EcoR124/EcoR124II comparison
also showed that one additional four-residue repeat changes `N6` to `N7`.
Because the Gold cohort spans several Type I families, production does not
apply `4 aa = 1 bp` globally and does not interpret a monomeric AlphaFold
centroid distance as a DNA spacer. Instead, it searches the conserved protein
scaffold so family, helix length, repeat phase, hinge residues, and interaction
context are transferred together.

Candidate scaffold windows, neighbor counts, length penalties, and ensemble
weights were selected only on the frozen 346-protein development partition.
The selected remote rule normalizes each branch's
`identity * min(query coverage, subject coverage)` class votes, then combines
whole-HsdS top-5 and scaffold top-20 probabilities at 70:30. Exact ties retain
the whole-HsdS winner. Close agreeing TRDs and the calibrated close whole-HsdS
1NN gate remain above this branch.

The molecular-ruler correction is deliberately narrower than the scaffold
model. It requires query and nearest whole-HsdS reference TAEL-like repeat
counts to be exactly two and three (in either direction), a one-repeat
difference, and a canonical reference label of two repeats=`N6` or three
repeats=`N7`. A close-full refinement additionally requires >=90% identity and
>=80% reciprocal coverage and can only refine an already eligible close-full
call. A remote refinement requires >=70% identity, >=80% reciprocal coverage,
and ensemble vote margin <=0.40; it always remains non-eligible. Direct repeat
priors without a canonical reference pair caused losses and were rejected.

Complete-motif comparisons below use the frozen paired cohort of 650 queries
for which the v1 half-site benchmark emitted both TRDs (334 development; 316
test). Regenerating the physically separated TRD searches recovered one
additional weak R0285 T1 hit, yielding 651 operational calls, but both R0285
half-sites were incorrect. To prevent a half-site coverage change from being
attributed to the spacer model, all paired spacer ablations retain the common
650-query denominator. On the operational cohort the corresponding numerators
are unchanged: exact scaffold 298/651, scaffold plus TAEL 300/651, and TRD90
246/651.

| Holdout | Spacer model | Spacer accuracy | Complete motif, common called cohort |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Exact LOO | weighted whole-HsdS 5NN | 414/689 (60.09%) | 289/650 (44.46%) |
| Exact LOO | + conserved scaffold 70:30 | 426/689 (61.83%) | 298/650 (45.85%) |
| Exact LOO | + conservative TAEL ruler | **429/689 (62.26%)** | **300/650 (46.15%)** |
| TRD90 | weighted whole-HsdS 5NN | 383/689 (55.59%) | 219/650 (33.69%) |
| TRD90 | + conserved scaffold 70:30 | 406/689 (58.93%) | 246/650 (37.85%) |
| TRD90 | + conservative TAEL ruler | **407/689 (59.07%)** | **246/650 (37.85%)** |

Frozen-split final counts were exact LOO 222/346 development and 207/343 test
spacers, with 159/334 and 141/316 complete motifs. TRD90 counts were 205/346
and 202/343 spacers, with 130/334 and 116/316 complete motifs. TAEL changed
three exact-LOO spacer calls and one TRD90 call: all four were wins and none
were losses. R0237 and R0262 also became complete-motif wins; R0688's
half-sites were incorrect, so its spacer correction did not change complete-
motif accuracy.

The improvement remains positive as homologous TRD components are removed at
progressively looser thresholds. The final all-reference spacer counts were
398 at TRD80, 389 at TRD70, 361 at TRD60, and 378 at TRD50, versus weighted-
5NN counts of 379, 354, 352, and 356. Frozen-test counts changed from 188 to
198, 178 to 193, 182 to 183, and 183 to 185. The gain narrows for the most
remote queries but does not reverse.

A direct nine-feature alpha-helix model was also tested. It reweighted the top
15 whole-HsdS homologs by the RMS z-distance of Pro/Gly fraction, Pro/Gly
count, and Chou-Fasman helix-run length in three central/junction windows. It
reached 427/689 exact and 399/689 TRD90 spacers, but only 293/650 and 234/650
complete motifs. A development-selected three-way spacer ensemble reached
431/689 exact and 406/689 TRD90 spacers but reduced TRD90 complete motifs to
241/650. Both were rejected because the 70:30 conserved-scaffold model was
better on the predeclared strict complete-motif objective. Results are recorded
in `type1s_structure_spacer_ablation.tsv`.

The scaffold ensemble and remote TAEL ruler are explicitly remote evidence.
Their provenance identity and coverage can never promote a prediction to the
`high`/eligible tier. The close-full ruler preserves an existing high gate but
cannot create one. Only exact Gold matches and the validated close-homolog
gates can enter that tier.

## PF01420 boundary ablation

The official Pfam `Methylase_S` profile (`PF01420.26`, model length 168;
HMM SHA-256
`f470db168742788a8dba01f9b8056d16990b239fb454ee10f18c2cddc4664ecf`)
was tested as a more direct domain-boundary alternative. At its curated GA
threshold, only 524/689 proteins (76.1%) produced exactly two non-overlapping
domains; 144 produced one, two produced three, and 19 produced none. The HMM
domains were broad (median alignment lengths 169 and 165 aa), while the fixed
specificity cores were 80 and 67 aa and covered only about 46.5% and 42.8% of
the HMM domains.

On the same 524 queries, using the current 689-reference fixed database versus
HMM-envelope databases gave the following TRD90 complete-motif results:

- fixed cores: 143/502 correct calls (28.49% precision);
- HMM envelopes: 143/523 correct calls (27.34% precision).

Thus the HMM emitted 21 extra calls without increasing the number of correct
complete motifs. It also increased identical-sequence spacer-conflict groups
from one to four per position. `PF01420` was therefore rejected as a mandatory
boundary replacement; it may still be useful later as an architecture/QC
signal. The official model came from the
[InterPro PF01420 API](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/interpro/wwwapi/entry/pfam/PF01420?annotation=hmm)
and is distributed under the
[InterPro data license](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/interpro/about/license/).

## Strict TRD90 holdout and final confidence calibration

An additional leakage control connected two references whenever either v1
TRD aligned at >=90% identity and >=80% reciprocal query/subject coverage.
All members of the query component were then removed from its TRD1, TRD2,
cross-position, whole-HsdS, and scaffold searches. This produced 550 components (498
singletons; maximum size 30) among the 689 references.

| Output | Exact-sequence LOO precision / coverage | TRD90 precision / coverage |
|---|---:|---:|
| Left half-site | 83.6% / 98.0% | 80.1% / 98.0% |
| Right half-site | 78.1% / 96.2% | 71.0% / 96.2% |
| Both half-sites | 66.9% / 94.3% | 58.3% / 94.3% |
| Spacer | 62.3% / 100% | 59.1% / 100% |
| Complete motif | 46.2% / 94.3% | 37.8% / 94.3% |

The old proportional-window baseline reached 21.2% complete-motif precision
under the same TRD90 holdout, versus 37.8% for the final model. This table uses
the frozen common half-site cohort described above; the single additional weak
operational call is incorrect and does not change any numerator. The stricter
analysis shows that remote-family calls must remain exploratory rather than
being presented as calibrated high-confidence predictions.

The final non-exact `high`/`type1s_prediction_eligible` rule therefore requires
both selected TRDs and the spacer source to have >=90% identity and >=80%
query **and** subject coverage. On all-reference exact-sequence LOO this rule
gave:

- half-sites: 97/97 correct (100% precision; 14.1% coverage);
- complete motif: 86/86 correct (100% precision; 12.5% coverage).

TRD90 yields zero calls at this threshold by construction: without a close
Gold homolog, the implementation abstains from the eligible tier. Exact
protein matches remain separately labeled `known`. Lower-evidence predictions
remain visible with half-site, spacer, and provenance columns for hypothesis
generation.

The historical v1.3 spacer fallback was selected after a direct ablation.
Allowing every
agreeing TRD pair to override whole-HsdS evidence gave 372/689 correct spacers
and 266/650 correct complete motifs in exact LOO, and 329/689 plus 195/650
under TRD90. Restricting TRD consensus to two close homologs improved those
counts to 381/689 and 271/650, and to 335/689 and 199/650. The v1.3 fallback
uses the top five whole-HsdS hits and votes by
`identity * min(query coverage, subject coverage)`, reaching 414/689 spacers
and 289/650 complete motifs in exact LOO, and 383/689 plus 219/650 under TRD90.
The v1.4 conserved-scaffold ensemble and TAEL ruler then improved these counts
to 429/689 and 300/650 under exact LOO, and 407/689 plus 246/650 under TRD90.

`k=5` and reciprocal-coverage weighting were chosen only on a deterministic
346-protein development partition (seed `20260715`) among k=3, 5, 7, and 10
and unweighted/bitscore/reciprocal variants. After freezing the rule, the
343-protein untouched partition improved from 180 to 202 correct exact-LOO
spacers and from 125/316 to 136/316 complete motifs. Under TRD90 it improved
from 162 to 192 spacers and from 93/316 to 105/316 complete motifs. Weighted
and scaffold votes plus the remote TAEL ruler are never promoted to `high`.
The close-full TAEL refinement corrected the two remaining exact-LOO eligible
errors without creating new eligible calls, producing the final 86/86 result.

Early simple central/linker alternatives did not justify extra complexity:
central 25-70%
and 30-66% one-neighbor searches reached 375/689 and 363/689 exact-LOO spacer
accuracy, respectively; three connected conserved regions reached 372/687;
and length/composition/helix-feature kNN reached 321/689. All were below the
whole-HsdS weighted vote. The later conserved-scaffold sequence ensemble is a
different top-20 class-vote model and passed the frozen complete-motif
objective reported above.

BLASTP and DIAMOND produced identical predictions for every call in the former,
broader high-confidence set (98 exact-LOO and 6 TRD90 calls). The backend
fallback therefore did not change eligible calls; the conservative threshold
was selected to address biological generalization, not backend disagreement.

Strict-holdout summary values are also stored in
`type1s_trd90_results.tsv`; spacer-model development/test and ablation counts
for the historical v1.3 fallback are stored in `type1s_spacer_ablation.tsv`.
Final scaffold, helix, combined-model, TAEL-ruler, and stricter-holdout results
are stored in `type1s_structure_spacer_ablation.tsv`; the four individual
ruler corrections and their gates are stored in `type1s_tael_ruler_cases.tsv`.
